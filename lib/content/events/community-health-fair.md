The Community Health Fair offers free health screenings and flu shots for all ages. Come for blood pressure and glucose checks, vaccinations, and information on nutrition, mental health resources, and insurance enrollment—no insurance or ID required for basic services.

## Overview {#overview}

The **Community Health Fair** is held at the Downtown Community Center in partnership with the city health department and local hospitals. The goal is to make preventive care and health information accessible to everyone. All basic screenings and flu shots are **free**; no insurance or ID is required. Staff can help you understand results and refer you to follow-up care if needed.

## Screenings and services {#screenings}

- **Blood pressure and glucose** — Quick checks to help you track your numbers. Results are explained on the spot.
- **Flu shots** — Available for adults and children (parent/guardian consent for minors). No appointment needed.
- **Health information** — Tables on nutrition, mental health resources, and enrollment in insurance and benefit programs. Staff can help you see if you qualify for low-cost or free coverage.
- **Referrals** — If a screening suggests follow-up, staff can help connect you to a clinic or primary care provider and assist with scheduling.

Screenings are first-come, first-served. Wait times may vary. The fair does not replace emergency care; for emergencies, call 911.

## Location and parking {#location-parking}

The fair is at the **Downtown Community Center**, 456 Main Street. The building is accessible by ramp and elevator. Free parking is available in the lot next to the center; additional street parking is nearby. The center is on bus routes 2, 4, and 7. The event runs from 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM.
