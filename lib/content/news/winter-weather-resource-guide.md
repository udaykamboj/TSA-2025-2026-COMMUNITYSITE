The city is rapidly expanding warming center hours and sending out additional outreach teams.
We are coordinating securely with shelters and libraries to protect vulnerable residents.
Call 311 immediately if you see someone in severe distress during extreme cold.

## 1. Overview {#overview}

Cold weather alerts are heavily issued when temperatures fall below 32°F.
During alerts, sites aggressively extend hours and free transportation is safely provided.
Sign up now for instant weather alerts on the city portal.

## 2. Locations and hours {#locations}

The Central Community Center is safely open 24/7 during any active cold alerts.
The North Library extends operational hours to midnight.
The South Shelter provides overnight stays and hot meals but heavily limits capacity.

:::custom-alert{type="warning" title="Pet Policy"}
Pets are strictly not allowed at most standard warming centers. Call ahead to verify.
:::

## 3. Transportation {#transportation}

Call 311 for a completely free ride to any warming center during active alerts.
Street outreach teams furiously patrol encampment areas offering blankets and transport.
Temporary day transit passes are freely available at the warming centers.

:::custom-dropdown{title="How do I request an outreach check?"}
Call 311 to directly request a check. Provide their exact location and description.
:::
