The city council has unanimously approved a massive five-year infrastructure improvement plan.
We will actively modernize key public facilities and improve transportation networks.
This heavily relies on existing funding streams rather than raising new taxes.

## 1. Key projects {#projects}

Over 40 miles of arterial roads will be fully repaved in the first two years.
Three aging bridges will immediately receive critical structural upgrades.
Two brand new multipurpose community centers will be built in underserved neighborhoods.

:::custom-card{title="Riverfront Expansion" image="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1486406146926-c627a92ad1ab?w=800&h=500&fit=crop" buttonText="View Park Plans" buttonLink="/infrastructure/parks"}
We are expanding the beautiful riverfront trail by 4.5 miles and adding new playground equipment to five parks.
:::

## 2. Project Timeline {#timeline}

2026 focuses heavily on design, procurement, and initial road contracts.
2027 and 2028 will see major construction phases and bridge work begin.
All community centers will officially open to the public in 2029.

## 3. How to get involved {#get-involved}

Attend quarterly public briefings at City Hall to stay informed.
Sign up for specific email updates for projects happening in your exact district.
Report hazardous potholes in your neighborhood via the 311 app to expedite paving.

:::custom-alert{type="info" title="Funding Secured"}
Funding is fully secured through federal grants, state matches, and existing capital budgets.
:::
