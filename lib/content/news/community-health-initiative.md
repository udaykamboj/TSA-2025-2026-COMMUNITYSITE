A new health initiative aims to drastically improve access to healthcare for all residents.
The city is rolling out mobile clinics, free telemedicine options, and monthly health fairs.
The primary goal is to reduce barriers to preventive care and same-day medical advice.

## 1. Services offered {#services}

Mobile clinics will travel to libraries and senior housing on a rotating, weekly schedule.
Free telemedicine video visits provide immediate care for non-urgent issues.
Monthly local health fairs will offer free screenings and vaccination shots.

:::custom-card{title="Telemedicine Access" image="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1576091160399-112ba8d25d1d?w=800&h=500&fit=crop" buttonText="Book a Video Visit" buttonLink="/health/telemedicine/book"}
Talk to a clinician online for free. Available Monday through Friday, 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM.
:::

## 2. Locations and hours {#locations}

Mobile clinic schedules are posted weekly on the city website.
No appointments are ever required for mobile clinic vaccinations.
Health fair dates are heavily advertised at the start of each month.

:::custom-alert{type="error" title="Emergency Services"}
This initiative does not provide emergency care. Call 911 for life-threatening emergencies.
:::

## 3. Eligibility {#eligibility}

The initiative is proudly open to all city residents.
You strictly do not need insurance to access basic screenings or telemedicine.
Interpretation services are rapidly available in multiple languages.

:::custom-dropdown{title="Do I need an ID?"}
No! You do not need to show an ID to receive basic services or flu shots.
:::
