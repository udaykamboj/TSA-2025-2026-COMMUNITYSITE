The city is partnering with developers to build mixed-income housing across five neighborhoods.
Priority will be given strictly to families and essential workers.
This is the largest single commitment to affordable rental housing in the city's history.

## 1. Overview {#overview}

The Affordable Housing Expansion will rapidly create 500 new rental units.
All sites will be built to the highest energy-efficiency standards.
Developers have firmly committed to local hiring during construction.

## 2. Project locations {#locations}

Northside Commons will feature 120 units near the Transit Station.
East Gate Village will provide 100 units walking distance to local schools.
Downtown Heights will create 115 units geared specifically toward essential workers.

:::custom-alert{type="info" title="Rent Levels"}
Rent levels will be set tightly according to area median income (AMI) and unit size.
:::

## 3. Eligibility and application {#eligibility}

Households earning 30–80% of the area median income may qualify.
Families, healthcare workers, teachers, and veterans will receive lottery preference.
A centralized application portal opens officially in March 2026.

:::custom-dropdown{title="Will there be family-sized units?"}
Yes. Units will heavily include one-, two-, and three-bedroom options with modern amenities.
:::
