## 1. Register to vote {#register}

You can register to vote online, by mail, or in person at the DMV.
You must provide your name, address, and valid state ID.

:::custom-card{title="Online Registration" buttonText="Register to Vote Online" buttonLink="/voting/register"}
Registering online is the fastest method. You will need your state-issued ID.
:::

## 2. Check your status {#status}

Confirm that you are registered and locate your assigned polling place.
Checking early helps avoid surprises on Election Day.

:::custom-button{href="/voting/check-status"}
Check Voter Status
:::

## 3. Absentee and mail-in voting {#absentee}

If you cannot vote in person, request an absentee or mail-in ballot early.
Deadlines to request and return ballots are strictly enforced.

:::custom-alert{type="info" title="Early Voting"}
In-person early voting is available at select locations. Check the schedule before Election Day.
:::

:::custom-dropdown{title="When is the registration deadline?"}
Deadlines are typically 15 to 30 days before an election. Check the state website for exact dates.
:::

:::custom-dropdown{title="Do I need ID at the polls?"}
Most states require photo ID at the polls, such as a driver's license or passport.
:::

:::custom-dropdown{title="I moved. Do I need to re-register?"}
Yes. You must update your address so you are assigned the correct ballot and polling place.
:::
