## 1. File a complaint {#file-complaint}

Report unsafe living conditions, heating issues, or pests.
You can file a complaint online or by calling 311.

:::custom-card{title="Emergency Repairs" buttonText="Call 311 Now" buttonLink="tel:311"}
If you have no heat, a gas leak, or a major water leak, report it immediately to 311. The city responds to emergencies 24/7.
:::

:::custom-button{href="/submit-complaint"}
File a Complaint Online
:::

## 2. Housing standards {#housing-standards}

Rental units must meet basic health and safety standards.
Landlords must provide adequate heat, hot water, working locks, and pest control.

:::custom-alert{type="warning" title="Withholding Rent"}
Do not withhold rent without legal advice. Rules vary and mistakes can affect your rights.
:::

## 3. Get help {#get-help}

Tenant support organizations can help you navigate the complaint process.
Free or low-cost legal aid is available for eligible residents.

:::custom-dropdown{title="What happens after I file?"}
The city may inspect the property. If violations are found, the landlord is ordered to correct them.
:::

:::custom-dropdown{title="Can my landlord retaliate?"}
No. It is illegal for a landlord to retaliate against you for filing a code complaint.
:::
