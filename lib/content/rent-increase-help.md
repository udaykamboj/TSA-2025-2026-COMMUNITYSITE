## 1. Understand your rights {#understand-your-rights}

In many areas, rent-stabilized or rent-controlled units have limits on how much and how often rent can be increased. Landlords must usually give advance notice before the increase takes effect. Even in unregulated units, landlords typically must give notice as required by state or local law and cannot raise rent in a discriminatory or retaliatory manner.

### Required notice period

| Conditions | Required notice |
|---|---|
| You've lived in the unit less than one year and you don't have lease term of one year or longer | 30 days |
| You've lived in the unit more than one year but less than two years | 60 days |
| You've lived in the unit for more than two years | 90 days |

Check local tenant laws for your area: rules vary by city and state. Some places have caps on annual increases, require justification for large increases, or give tenants the right to a lease renewal under certain conditions.

## 3. Dispute an increase {#dispute}

If you believe an increase is illegal or improper, you may be able to file a challenge or complaint. Document the notice you received and any relevant lease terms. 

Tenant organizations can help you understand your options, deadlines, and the evidence you need. Legal aid may be available if you are low-income.

:::custom-button{href="/main/services/rent-increase-help"}
File a complaint online
:::

:::alert{type="warning" title="Pay your rent"}
Do not refuse to pay rent without legal advice—that can lead to eviction; instead, pay the amount you believe is correct (e.g., the old rent) and seek guidance on how to contest the increase properly.
:::

## 4. Rental assistance {#assistance}

Rental assistance programs may help eligible households with past-due rent or ongoing affordability. Eligibility is often based on income and household size; some programs target families with children, seniors, or people with disabilities. 

:::card{title="Cash help during an emergency"}
You may be able to get cash help if you're in danger of losing your housing. This help is called "Emergency Assistance" or a "One Shot Deal."
:::

:::card{title="Services to help you keep your housing"}
If you're at risk of becoming homeless, Homebase providers can help you get support fast.
:::
